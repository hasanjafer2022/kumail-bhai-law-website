PROPERTY CASES

The Indian Constitution does not specifically contain provisions solely dedicated to property cases. However, it includes various articles and amendments that touch upon property rights and their regulation.

The key provisions related to property in the Indian Constitution are as follows :

1. Article 300A: This article guarantees the right to property as a constitutional right. It states that no person shall be deprived of their property except by the authority of law.

2. Article 19(1)(f): This provision falls under the Fundamental Rights of the Indian Constitution and guarantees the right to acquire, hold, and dispose of
property. However, this right is not absolute and can be subjected to reasonable restrictions imposed by the state.

3. Article 31: This article, which was repealed by the 44th Amendment Act in 1978, originally protected the right to property as a fundamental right.
It stated that no person shall be deprived of their property except for public purposes and on the payment of just compensation.

4.Various state-level laws: Different Indian states have enacted their own laws related to property, such as tenancy laws, land revenue laws, and laws governing land disputes.
These laws may vary from state to state.

The Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation, and Resettlement Act, 2013 (Land Acquisition Act): This law provides the framework
for the acquisition of land by the government for public purposes, such as infrastructure development. It ensures fair compensation, rehabilitation, and resettlement of affected persons.

It is important to note that the interpretation and implementation of property laws in India are influenced by judicial decisions and case law, which have evolved over time.
It is advisable to consult a legal professional or refer to the latest statutes and judicial pronouncements for precise and up-to-date information on property cases in India.
